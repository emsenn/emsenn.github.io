Kyriarchy

Kyriarchy is a method of social order in which violence is seen as a legitimate means of achieving a resolution between two people.

The term was coined by Elisabeth Schussler Fiorenza in 1992.

Over time, I've come to use kyriarchy as the most general descriptor for any social order based on domination, regardless of its mechanisms.

One benefit of the word is that it encorporates intersectionality at the foundation of its conception of social orders.
