January 1st

Congress of Racial Equality founed by James Farmer, Chicago, Illinois, U.S.A., 1942

On this date in 1942, in Chicago, Illinois, U.S.A., James Farmer founded the Congress of Racial Equality. The group's membership largely came from the pacifist group Fellowship of Reconciliation. They were inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's strategy of non-violence and sought to apply it in abolishing segregation. Notably, the group was 28 men and 22 women, with nearly 2/3rds of its founding membership being white.

Oscar Grant III, shot by police, Oakland, California, U.S.A., 2009

Oscar Grant III was shot by police in Oakland, California, U.S.A., by officers affiliated with the city's public transport, while unarmed and held in a prone position. His shooting was captured on video by bystanders.
