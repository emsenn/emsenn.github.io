Looking at Portland's new pro-Indigenous directives

  • Note taken on [2021-01-06 Wed 13:28]
    Back on December 12th, the Portland City Council approved a series of directives designed to facilitate a better relationship between the city and its Indigenous residents and neighbors. The biggest directive is an official land acknowledgement that the city will give sometime this year. The directives also establish Indigenous Peoples Day as a paid holiday for city employees, an exploration of park fee waivers for Indigenous ceremonies, strategizing to increase how many Indigenous employees the city has, creating a recurring training for all city employees, and departments have to create plans on developing collaboration with sovereign tribal nations in providing services to urban Indigenous communities.

    While any appreciation of the need to change our relationship is good, I'm unsure if Portland appreciates that… well, it's a city, and it's a bit telling of the relationship they want to have with Indigenous communities that "city departments" and "sovereign tribal nations" are supposed to communicate as peers. I see glimmers of a future that's much like the relationship between anthropologists and Indigenous peoples, where any old colonial student feels fully entitled to consult with the leaders of tribal nations.

Source

Hundreds wear ribbon skirts after Saskatchewan girl shamed for hers

  • Note taken on [2021-01-06 Wed 12:11]
    Isabella Kulak, a 10-year-old member of the Cote First Nation in eastern Saskatchewan, wore a ribbon skirt to her school for a "formal day," but was told her mismatched skirt and shrit were inappropriate, suggesting Isabella should have worn a store-bought dress. In support, hundreds of Indigenous people have posted photographs of themselves in their ribbon skirts, including in formal settings.
