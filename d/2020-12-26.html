Dennis Schep: "Freeing space: building worlds outside of state and capital"

  • Note taken on [2020-12-26 Sat 11:32]
    I'm not going to bother forming a well-structured critique of this piece; it isn't worth it. The article is neo-pioneer nonsense. Building worlds outside of the state is something Indigenous people do regardless of where they live, inside kyriarchism or not. Colonizers moving "outside the state," is usually just a LARP of anarchism, supported by larger systems of colonialism than the closest state. Going out into the woods to make art isn't building a better world, it's moving where resource scarcity isn't too severe to secure personal prosperity, which is the same thing colonials have been doing for centuries.

    Take a lesson from me and other Indigenous people, who are building our indigenity within the shoals of settler-colonialism: You don't need to move anywhere to build a better world, you just have to move your hands.

    And, ignoring the neo-pioneerism expressed, I think solutions like those proposed by Dennis gravely underestimate how much of our resources we've been using. Moving out of a city isn't going to do anything on its own, even if we all stopped making trash and using water and energy. People need to be actively making soil, undoing generations of damage to our freshwater resevoirs and topsoil, not pursuing personal passions. There's a reason I'm mostly active on the Web during rainstorms and the winter: I'm busy making soil the rest of the time!

    To put it really clearly: moving to another place because it has resources you don't have where you are isn't anything different than kyriarchism. If you want to build a better world, imitate the people who live in a better world. Decolonize, indigenize.

    Land back, water back, medicine back, ceremony back.
